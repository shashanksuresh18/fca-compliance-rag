## 1. Overview and Regulatory Basis

This policy sets out [Bank Name]'s obligations under the Senior Managers and Certification Regime (SM&CR), introduced into UK law by the Financial Services (Banking Reform) Act 2013 and amended by the Financial Services and Markets Act 2023. The SM&CR is regulated by the Financial Conduct Authority (FCA) and the Prudential Regulation Authority (PRA).

The SM&CR applies to all FCA-authorised firms and is designed to improve culture and accountability within financial institutions by ensuring that individuals who hold key senior management functions are appropriately approved, and that all relevant employees meet and continue to meet a fitness and propriety standard.

## 2. The Conduct Rules

The SM&CR Conduct Rules apply to all employees of the firm. They are divided into two tiers.

### 2.1 Individual Conduct Rules (Tier 1)

All employees covered by SM&CR must adhere to the following Individual Conduct Rules as defined in the FCA's COCON sourcebook:

1. **Rule 1 — Act with Integrity**: You must act with integrity in carrying out your functions.
2. **Rule 2 — Act with Due Skill**: You must act with due skill, care and diligence.
3. **Rule 3 — Be Open with Regulators**: You must be open and cooperative with the FCA, PRA and other regulators.
4. **Rule 4 — Treat Customers Fairly**: You must pay due regard to the interests of customers and treat them fairly.
5. **Rule 5 — Observe Proper Market Standards**: You must observe proper standards of market conduct.

### 2.2 Senior Manager Conduct Rules (Tier 2)

In addition to the Individual Conduct Rules above, Senior Managers must comply with:

1. **SC1 — Effective Business Control**: You must take reasonable steps to ensure that the business of the firm for which you are responsible is controlled effectively.
2. **SC2 — Compliance with Regulatory Requirements**: You must take reasonable steps to ensure that the business of the firm for which you are responsible complies with the relevant requirements and standards of the regulatory system.
3. **SC3 — Delegation**: You must take reasonable steps to ensure that any delegation of your responsibilities is to an appropriate person and that you oversee the discharge of the delegated responsibility effectively.
4. **SC4 — Disclosure to Regulators**: You must disclose appropriately any information of which the FCA or PRA would reasonably expect notice.

## 3. Senior Management Functions (SMFs)

Senior Managers must be pre-approved by the FCA or PRA before taking up their role. The firm must submit a Form A application for each SMF holder.

Key SMFs at this firm include:

| SMF | Role Title | Regulator |
|-----|-----------|-----------|
| SMF1 | Chief Executive | PRA/FCA |
| SMF3 | Executive Director | PRA/FCA |
| SMF16 | Compliance Oversight | FCA |
| SMF17 | Money Laundering Reporting Officer | FCA |
| SMF27 | Partner | FCA |

Senior Managers must maintain a current **Statement of Responsibilities (SoR)** describing their remit. SoRs must be submitted to the regulator and kept up to date within 30 days of any material change.

## 4. The Certification Regime

Employees who are not Senior Managers but whose roles could cause significant harm to the firm or its customers must be certified annually as fit and proper. This is the **Certification Regime**.

The firm's HR and Compliance teams are jointly responsible for conducting annual fit and proper assessments. These assessments must consider:

- **Honesty, integrity and reputation**: No relevant criminal convictions, regulatory findings, or civil findings.
- **Competence and capability**: Holding appropriate qualifications for the role.
- **Financial soundness**: No patterns of financial distress that could create conflicts of interest.

Certificates must be issued in writing and retained for a minimum of 6 years.

## 5. Breach Reporting and Non-Compliance

Any breach of the Conduct Rules by a Senior Manager or certified employee must be reported to the FCA within 7 business days of the firm becoming aware under SUP 10C.14.

Internal breaches identified through the whistleblowing channel or HR investigation process must be escalated to the Compliance Oversight SMF (SMF16) within 24 hours.

Failure to report a breach is itself a breach of SC4 (Disclosure to Regulators).
